About me

I am 24, Indian, female and an engineer. Just another millenial trying to carve her place in the world.

Most of who I am comes from my father, and some very nerdy friends. I am a profound lover of literature, language, Europe and music that reminds me of good times. I am also a strongly opinionated and stubborn freak, but fortunately, I never learnt to apologise for who I am, thanks to the influence of some people that I love the most. I love to teach, my strengths being mathematics and my imperfect love being French and piano.

This website for me is my little peace mission, to remind people (and myself) that there's more to life than we settle for, there's more to evenings than TV, there's more to weekends than sleep. It's to remind us that sometime, somewhere in life, we have all felt truly at home, like there's nowhere else we'd rather be, and that we should never stop chasing that.

This, below, is a tribute to my friends, people who have transferred some very important values to me, values that keep me up and standing.

This is exactly who I was before I met Mr. Patience. He keeps me grounded to earth, acts as my anchor and reminds me everytime I am angry that it's not ok to pull someone's hair out because they accidentaly stepped on my foot.

This friend of mine could write a book about how best to give a fuck. When he starts talking about things he loves or things he would like to change, he makes you feel like there's more meaning in the world than you ever found.

My soul-brother from another mother. I've never seen someone so comfortable in their own skin, so unapologetically being themselves. My partner in art, love and crime, if I had to describe him in a word-it would be 'petrichor'.

This friend contradicts everything I believe in. He teaches me that no matter how much you care, how passionate you are, every once in a while you should sit back and marvel at the meaninglessness of everything you claim is important.
