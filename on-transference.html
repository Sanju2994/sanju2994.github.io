On Transference

By Rama Tentu

Yesterday an old dear friend suggested that we watch the movie ‘Touched with Fire’. Quite unexpectedly I didn’t know I signed up for something surreal. I won’t be spoiling the movie for you, however I will tell you what it can make you feel. The movie skillfully portrays the struggle of a bipolar man and a bipolar woman to relate to one another and the rest of the world. I realized that it is hard to imagine what it is like to be in the maniac episodes of ecstasy and depression.

There are multiple references to Van Gogh and his art. I remember that in one of our literature classes we were asked to look at a painting of a Chair by Van Gogh as part of a self portrait exercise. Van Gogh’s painting was a personification of himself, it was an interesting concept. I looked into several other paintings of his like Starry night, the Yellow house and a few self portraits. I was appalled to know that he cut his ears during an episode of hallucination and lost consciousness with no recollection of the event.

The painting of a screaming man above gives a glimpse into the painful and contorted state of Van Gogh’s mind. The characters in the movie relate to his suffering, art and brilliance. It left me feeling empty to think how misunderstood are people who suffer from mental health illness, how the society fails to empathize with them and how easily the world can become lonely for them.

The melancholy of the music, the lighting effects, the raw emotions of Luna & Emily and lingering in air the fact that life is a sour grape for some more than others is just sad.

I will take some time to let it sink into my heart and brim until I have no choice but to stop.
